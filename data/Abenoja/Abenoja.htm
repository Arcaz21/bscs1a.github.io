"The First Half of My ‘Kinabuhing USePian"

I felt the nostalgia when I rode via Metro Shuttle bus. Leaving from home and exploring your own dreams are a very important part of maturing. For some, the physical separation from home is a difficult transition to college life. During the onset of the first half of classes in USeP, I was still worried, somewhat, of the step I was going to have to make in regards to the quality of work that I was going to have to do in order to pass with decent grades. In high school I had made procrastination an immense part of my life. It continued throughout the semester and found myself in deep voids that were extremely stressful.

Not only did the schoolwork affect my life, but my personal life was affected as well. My addiction to computer games had almost been a year before I made my way to college. Another thing that I realized is that privacy is always available at home. There is always somewhere to read, write, listen to music, or to just rest. Dorm life is a harsh reality to college; it’s life-changing to me especially that I’m a neophyte.

Demanding teachers, out-of-this-world exams, and frantic schedules. Everything is putting weight to my pressure. And as a freshman, adjustments to these are hard to achieve. Satan is seducing me to partake the forbidden fruit of sin. But all of these experiences with the first semester in the University, I experienced to be difficult with regards to maintain of my passing grade even though it cost my allowance. And I learned to adjust the future events of my life. And hopefully, it will lead my feet to the right path towards success.
